<p><p>The mechanical vacuum pressure gauge market can be categorized into two main types: direct and indirect gauges. Direct gauges measure vacuum pressure through sensing elements that respond to pressure changes, providing immediate readings. Indirect gauges, on the other hand, determine vacuum pressure through secondary means, often involving differential measurements or related parameters. Direct gauges are typically favored for their simplicity and accuracy, while indirect gauges are used in cases where direct measurement is impractical or requires additional calibration.</p></p>
