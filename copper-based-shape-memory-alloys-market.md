<p><p>Copper-based shape memory alloys (SMAs) exhibit unique properties, transitioning between high-temperature austenite and low-temperature martensite phases. In the austenite phase, the alloy maintains a stable, rigid structure, ideal for high-temperature applications. In the martensite phase, it becomes malleable and can recover its original shape upon heating, suitable for low-temperature uses. These alloys are widely used in industries like aerospace, automotive, and biomedical devices due to their shape memory effect, superelasticity, and adaptability to varying thermal conditions.</p></p>
