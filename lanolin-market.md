<p><p>The lanolin market is primarily divided into anhydrous and hydrous lanolin types. Anhydrous lanolin is a concentrated, oil-like substance obtained by refining wool grease, making it suitable for various applications in cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and personal care products. Hydrous lanolin, on the other hand, contains water and is generally used in formulations requiring moisture retention, such as lotions and creams. Both types serve distinct roles in enhancing product performance, moisturizing properties, and skin compatibility across multiple industries.</p></p>
