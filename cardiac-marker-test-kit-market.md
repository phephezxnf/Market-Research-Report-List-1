<p><p>The cardiac marker test kit market is segmented into three main types: whole blood, serum, and plasma. Whole blood tests provide immediate results and are often used in emergency settings. Serum tests, derived from clotted blood, offer higher specificity for markers, making them suitable for laboratory analysis. Plasma tests, obtained from anticoagulated blood, preserve the proteins of interest, facilitating accurate measurements. Each type serves distinct diagnostic purposes, catering to healthcare providers' needs for timely and precise cardiac assessments.</p></p>
