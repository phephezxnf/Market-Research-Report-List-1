<p><p>The wrist orthoses market is divided into two primary types: dynamic orthotics and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to allow some movement while providing support and stability, making them suitable for rehabilitation and functional recovery. They often incorporate adjustable components to accommodate varying levels of activity. In contrast, static orthotics are designed to immobilize the wrist to promote healing and prevent further injury. They are typically used in post-operative care or for severe conditions requiring complete immobilization.</p></p>
