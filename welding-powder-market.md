<p><p>The welding powder market is categorized into several types based on the metal composition used in welding processes. Ferrous-based powders are primarily used for steel applications, offering strength and durability. Silver-based powders provide excellent electrical conductivity and corrosion resistance, ideal for specialized applications. Copper-based powders are known for their thermal conductivity, suitable for electrical components. Aluminum-based powders are lightweight and corrosion-resistant, while nickel-based powders are used for high-temperature applications. Titanium-based powders are prized for their strength-to-weight ratio, with other types encompassing various specialty alloys and materials.</p></p>
