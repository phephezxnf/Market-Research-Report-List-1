<p><p>Electromagnetic Flowmeters (EMF) are instruments used to measure the flow rate of conductive fluids. They operate on the principle of Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction, where a magnetic field is applied to a fluid flowing through a pipe, generating a voltage proportional to the flow velocity. EMFs are widely utilized in various industries, including water and wastewater, chemical processing, food and beverage, and pharmaceuticals, due to their ability to provide accurate and reliable measurements without pressure drop.</p><p>The Electromagnetic Flowmeter (EMF) Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.4% during the forecast period, driven by increasing demand for accurate flow measurement and the growth of various end-user industries.
