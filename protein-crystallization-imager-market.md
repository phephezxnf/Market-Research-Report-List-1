<p><p>The Protein Crystallization Imager market is divided into two main types: monochrome imaging and color imaging. Monochrome imaging utilizes black-and-white sensors to capture high-contrast images of protein crystals, enabling precise analysis of crystal structure and morphology. In contrast, color imaging employs sensors that capture a wider spectrum of light, providing detailed visual information about the crystallization process and potential impurities. Both types serve crucial roles in research and pharmaceutical development, facilitating enhanced understanding of protein behavior and characteristics.</p></p>
