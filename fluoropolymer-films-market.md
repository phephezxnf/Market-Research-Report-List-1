<p><p>The fluoropolymer films market comprises several key types, each with unique properties. Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) films offer high chemical resistance and thermal stability, making them ideal for insulation and coatings. Polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) films are known for their excellent durability and UV resistance, suitable for membrane applications. Fluorinated Ethylene Propylene (FEP) films provide ease of processing and are used in wiring and insulation. Perfluoroalkoxy Polymer (PFA) films offer similar benefits to PTFE but with better flow characteristics. Ethylene Tetrafluoroethylene (ETFE) films feature high clarity and chemical resistance, often used in architectural applications. Other types include specialized formulations for niche applications.</p></p>
