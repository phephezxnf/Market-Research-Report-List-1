<p><p>Wireless Mesh Networking Devices can be classified into two main types: Infrastructure and Ad-Hoc networks. Infrastructure mesh networks feature fixed nodes that provide a robust, stable framework for connectivity, ideal for urban or large-area applications. This type ensures reliable communication and centralized management. In contrast, Ad-Hoc networks consist of mobile nodes that dynamically connect without a pre-defined structure, making them suitable for temporary setups like disaster recovery or events. This flexibility allows rapid deployment and adaptable connectivity solutions.</p></p>
