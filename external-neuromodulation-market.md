<p><p>The external neuromodulation market comprises several key types, including Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation (TENS), Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS), and Respiratory Electrical Stimulation (RES). TENS uses low-voltage electrical currents to relieve pain by stimulating nerves through the skin. TMS employs magnetic fields to modulate neuronal activity, often used for depression treatment. RES targets respiratory muscles with electrical impulses to enhance breathing efficiency. Together, these technologies aim to improve patient outcomes across various medical conditions by altering neural and muscular functions.</p></p>
